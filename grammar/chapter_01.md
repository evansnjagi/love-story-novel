# The Empty Life (Grammar)
---

Good writting is not impressive words. It is the right words in the right order.

---

**Adverbs** are words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They often end in 'ly'. Example: silently, literally, lazily, etc. Use them sparingly because they are weak verbs.

Instead of writting: "he stood silently in the cold room" you can write "He stood still in the cold room".

---

Using **Sensory Language** (sight, touch, taste, smell, and sound) makes the reader experience the scene rather than read it. 

---

Use **Contrast** by placing two opposite ideas together to create an effect. 

Never just name a feeling or a condition. Make the reader experience it through the senses.

---

**Specificity** - The more specific your details, the more real your story feels. Specific desires makes unforgettable characters, vaque desires makes forgettable ones.

---

**Parallel structure** - When listing items, keep them in the same grammatical form.

---

**Rule** A character desire is as strong as the obstacles that stands in it's way. Show the cage before showing the bird wants to fly.
